Self-Regulation

Manages impulses, attention, and emotions via a cycle of self-monitoring, analysis and self-adjusting.

Sense-Making

Converts raw data into structured schemas by integrating metacognitive signals, addressing allocentric perspectives, reconciling discrepancies across inputs, and revising preconceptions, by exploring, reframing, and testing ideas.

Critical Thinking

Evaluates assertions by estimating the prior plausibility of the proposition, assessing source reliability, distinguishing between claim, evidence and inference as separate justificatory layers, and evaluating logical validity and evidential relevance.
